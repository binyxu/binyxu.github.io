Research Thesis

One loop: sense risk → verify externally → build autonomy.

Closed-Loop Autonomous Intelligence
From risk sensing to reliable, low-cost long-horizon autonomy
I frame intelligent-system capability as a closed loop: an agent senses its own state and environmental risk, maintains memory and consolidates skills, and uses external verification and risk feedback to stay reliable in deployment. My work follows one method across domains:
  1. Long-horizon autonomy: self-managed context (VISTA), agentic memory, and skill distillation that let agents manage state, accumulate experience, and internalize multi-agent workflows at lower cost.
  2. External auditing & verification: using external models (e.g., VLMs / CLIP) to audit untrusted models and separate poisoned from clean data.
  3. Risk discovery & feedback: revealing failure modes of deployed models and feeding failure cost back into automation decisions.
